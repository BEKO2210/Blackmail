How It Works

Understanding AEGIS — a client-side Dead Man's Switch.

What is a Dead Man's Switch?

A Dead Man's Switch is a mechanism that activates automatically when a person fails to perform a regular action. Originally used in trains and industrial equipment, the concept has been adapted for digital security: if you stop checking in regularly, pre-configured actions are triggered — in this case, the release of encrypted evidence to your trusted guardians.

The AEGIS System

1. Setup

You upload your evidence files and choose a strong passphrase. All encryption happens locally in your browser using AES-256-GCM. You also set a duress passphrase — a secondary code for emergencies.

2. Distribute

The passphrase is split into fragments using Shamir's Secret Sharing. Each guardian receives a kit containing their unique fragment and detailed instructions. No single guardian can decrypt alone.

3. Check-in

You check in regularly by entering your passphrase. This generates a cryptographic token that you send to your guardians, proving you are safe and active.

4. Duress Protection

If you are under coercion, entering the duress passphrase generates a token that appears identical but contains a hidden flag. Your guardians can detect this and know to escalate immediately.

5. Escalation

If check-ins stop, guardians follow a pre-defined escalation plan. They combine their fragments to reconstruct the passphrase and decrypt the evidence package.

The Guardian Concept

Guardians are trusted persons who each hold one fragment of the decryption key. They have three responsibilities: (1) regularly verify your check-in tokens, (2) detect duress signals, and (3) coordinate with other guardians during escalation. A configurable threshold ensures that a minimum number of guardians must cooperate — protecting against both single-point failures and betrayal.

Client-Side Encryption

AEGIS uses the Web Crypto API built into modern browsers. All encryption (AES-256-GCM) and key derivation (PBKDF2 with 600,000 iterations) happen locally. No plaintext data ever leaves your device. The encrypted package can be stored anywhere — cloud, USB, IPFS — without compromising security.

No Central Authority

AEGIS has no server, no database, no accounts, and no central point of failure. The system is fully distributed: you hold the encrypted data, your guardians hold the key fragments, and the check-in verification is purely cryptographic. No one — not even the developers — can access your data.

Security Notice
AEGIS is a tool, not a guarantee. Your security depends on: the strength of your passphrase, the trustworthiness of your guardians, the safe storage of the encrypted package, and regular check-ins. Losing your passphrase means losing access. If all guardian fragments are compromised, your data is exposed. Use AEGIS as one layer in a comprehensive security strategy.
